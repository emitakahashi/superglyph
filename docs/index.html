How many layers of meaning can we embed into a variable glyph? In this workshop, we will explore the conceptual and poetic potential of variable font axes. Taking a personal object as a starting point, participants will design a superglyph which translates its formal, textural, emotional and/or symbolic qualities into one or several conceptual variable axes.

2:20–2:30 (15min)
Exercise 01: Object Choose an object as a source of inspiration for your glyph and variable axis.


  1. Is it personal? Does it exist in your space?
  2. Is it inanimate or animate?
  3. Consider looking around you, through the photo gallery on your phone, Wikipedia Commons or Modem Studio's image collection if you're not sure where to start.

Outcome: Upload an image that best represents your object in your respective are.na channel. The image can either be found or self-authored.


2:20–2:30 (20min)
Exercise 02: Variable Axis

Develop at least one conceptual variable axis inspired by your chosen object.


  1. What would you like to suggest/reveal/communicate about your object?
  2. What aspect of your object is informing your axis / spectrum? (e.g.: form, action, condition, texture, emotion, function, quality, quantity, time, etc)
  3. Tktktktktk

Outcome: Determine the following:

  1. Name of the variable axis
  2. Name of the styles on variable axis


2:20–2:33 (20min)
Exercise 03: Glyph

Determine the starting shape of your glyph. Start drawing it in Glyphs App.


  1. How does it relate to your object?
  2. Is it an existing glyph or letterform?
  3. Are you creating a new glyph?

Outcome: Starting shape drawn in Glyphs. Share your process in are.na (if you're comfortable).


2:20–2:33 (20min)
Exercise 04: Variable Glyph

Create the variable glyph which applies the conceptual axis onto the glyph you established in Exercises 2 & 3.


  1. How do each axis extreme manifests visually?
  2. Consider how motion will affect your glyph when transitioning from one state to another. Is it subtle, contrasted, intense, etc.?
  3. Tktktktktk

Outcome: Variable glyph drawn in Glyphs App



~~~~ 10 min break ~~~

2:20–2:33 (20min)
Final: Short Presentations

Share your variable axis and glyph with others!


  1. Willing participants give 1-2 min presentation of their object, variable axis, and final variable glyph.
  2. Please feel free to present your work how ever way you like. (Ex: using DINAMO Font Gauntlet to animate your glyph)
  3. Work in progress or process work is highly encouraged!
